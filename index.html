Skill Building for Future Ownership: This service acts as a "practicum" for pet care. By managing the daily needs of a trained dog for one week, users gain hands-on experience and confidence in their ability to care for an animal, helping them determine if they are ready for a permanent pet.
Lowering Barriers to Entry: Many people with anxiety may feel overwhelmed by the idea of a dog staying in their home immediately. A scheduled office visit provides a safe, controlled environment to test their comfort levels and build a bond with the staff and animals first.
Therapeutic Routine & Motivation: or individuals struggling with depression, a dog provides a gentle "nudge" toward a healthy routine. The need to provide the dog with regular walks and meals can help users re-establish a sense of purpose and structure in their daily lives.
Professional Social Support: This service connects users with both therapy animals and knowledgeable staff. It creates a community touchpoint where users can ask questions about animal behavior and mental health, reducing the feeling of isolation often associated with these conditions.
